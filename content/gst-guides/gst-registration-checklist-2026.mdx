Getting GST registration right the first time saves you weeks of back-and-forth with the department. Here's the checklist we run through with every client before we file.

## Before You Start

Confirm which threshold applies to you: ₹40 lakh turnover for goods, ₹20 lakh for services (₹20 lakh and ₹10 lakh respectively in special-category states). If you're below the threshold but want to register anyway, which is common for freelancers and D2C sellers, that's allowed too.

## Documents by Entity Type

- **Sole Proprietor**: PAN, Aadhaar, photograph, address proof, bank proof.
- **Partnership/LLP**: Partnership deed or LLP agreement, firm PAN, partner PAN/Aadhaar, DSC (LLP only).
- **Private Limited**: Certificate of Incorporation, MOA/AOA, director PAN/Aadhaar, DSC, board resolution.

See our full [documents checklist page](/gst-registration-documents) for the complete table, including HUF and foreign company requirements.

## The Five Mistakes That Cause Delays

1. Name or address mismatches between PAN and your address proof.
2. Blurry, oversized, or wrong-format document uploads.
3. Choosing the wrong constitution type (e.g., registering as a proprietor when you've actually incorporated an LLP).
4. Missing digital signature or e-sign from the authorised signatory.
5. Picking an incorrect HSN/SAC code for your principal business activity.

## What Happens After You File

You'll receive an ARN (Application Reference Number) immediately. Most straightforward applications complete Aadhaar-based e-KYC online with no physical visit. If everything checks out, GSTIN issuance typically takes 3 to 7 working days.

## Our Take

Most registration delays aren't the department being slow. They're avoidable document errors caught only after submission. Reviewing documents *before* filing, not after a clarification notice, is the single biggest lever for a fast approval.
